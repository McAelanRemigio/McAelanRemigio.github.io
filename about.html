About

I build decision-support systems for strategy and operations teams. My work focuses on turning messy institutional, operational, and research data into clear reporting systems, reusable workflows, and stakeholder-ready insights.

I am a junior at San Diego State University, majoring in Statistics with an emphasis in Data Science and minoring in Leadership Development. My background combines technical analytics, organizational leadership, and business-facing reporting work across higher education, public-sector research, and scientific data environments.

At SDSU Career Services, I build analytics systems that support institutional decision-making. I led labor market analysis across four California State University campuses to support a statewide workforce development proposal, contributing to a successful $2M grant award. My work has included KPI design, reporting infrastructure, data documentation, and translating fragmented operational exports into decision-ready outputs for leadership.

I also founded SDSU’s first data science organization, the Data Science Initiative, helping establish the initial data science community on campus and mentoring students into analytics and technical opportunities. For this leadership and service, I received the 2025 Daniel B. Nowak Outstanding Student Service Award, SDSU’s top university-wide student service honor.

Across roles with SDSU, the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, and the Vera C. Rubin Observatory, I have worked at the intersection of analytics, strategy, and execution. My goal is to build systems that do more than produce analysis — they help organizations understand what is happening, why it matters, and what to do next.

Current focus
