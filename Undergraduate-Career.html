What this is

SDSU Undergraduate Career is a living repository of applied analytics and reporting work developed alongside my undergraduate studies. Rather than functioning as a single polished project, it documents how I build reusable workflows for data cleaning, exploratory analysis, reporting, and decision support.

How it’s used

  • Documenting applied analytics projects that do not require full standalone case studies.
  • Tracking technical growth through reproducible notebooks, notes, and experiments.
  • Maintaining a transparent record of how unclear questions become structured workflows.
  • Supporting larger projects with reusable methods for analysis, reporting, and documentation.

Scope

The repository spans multiple semesters and includes work across data analysis, reporting, exploratory research, workflow design, and technical documentation. It reflects a broader pattern in my work: using data systems to clarify operational questions and support better decisions.

Focus areas

  • Applied analytics
  • Decision-support workflows
  • Reproducible reporting
  • Business intelligence foundations
  • Translating ambiguity into structure

Tools

Python · R · SQL · Jupyter · Data visualization · Documentation

Project type

Living archive · Technical development · Analytics workflow documentation

Why this matters

Strategy and operations work often begins with ambiguous questions, incomplete data, and unclear reporting needs. This repository shows how I approach that ambiguity: structure the problem, build reproducible analysis, document the workflow, and translate findings into usable outputs for future projects.

Connection to my broader work

This archive supports my broader focus on building decision-support systems for strategy and operations teams. It captures the technical habits behind that work: cleaning messy data, designing repeatable workflows, documenting assumptions, and turning analysis into formats that can be reused by stakeholders.
